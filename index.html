Why Early Screening Matters

Lung cancer is often called a "silent disease" because it typically doesn't show symptoms in its early stages — when treatment is most effective. Regular screening can catch cancer early and save lives.

What is LDCT Screening?

Low Dose Computed Tomography (LDCT) is a quick, painless scan that takes detailed pictures of your lungs. Here's what to expect:

1

Arrive at the Facility

Check in at the imaging center. The whole process typically takes less than 30 minutes.

2

Lie on the Table

You'll lie on a comfortable table that slides into a donut-shaped scanner. No needles or injections needed.

3

Hold Your Breath

The technician will ask you to hold your breath for a few seconds while the machine captures images of your lungs.

4

Get Your Results

Your doctor will receive the results and discuss them with you, usually within a few days.

Our Journey

From a single library walkthrough to school board presentations, Project Breathe Hope has been on the move. Here's every event we've hosted.

📍 Event #1 — Immersive Walkthrough at Milpitas Library

April 17, 2026
✅ 154 attendees confirmed

Our flagship event at the Milpitas Library featured interactive stations, a survivor wall, myth-busting displays, and free care packages. Community members of all ages walked through the LDCT screening process and learned how to assess their own risk.

📱 Watch our event TikTok recap: youtube.com/shorts/YMMl7ZWixsc

🏡 Event #2 — Immersive Walkthrough at Local Senior Center

Spring 2026

We brought our immersive walkthrough experience to a local senior center — one of the highest-risk populations for lung cancer. Seniors engaged with educational displays, completed risk assessments, and left with resource packets about local screening centers.

🏡 Event #3 — Second Senior Center Walkthrough

Spring 2026

We returned to another senior center with our refined walkthrough, featuring our colorful "Who Lung Cancer Why" poster and expanded educational materials. Seniors could walk the full simulated LDCT process and ask questions one-on-one.
