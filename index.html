Why Early Screening Matters

Lung cancer is often called a "silent disease" because it typically doesn't show symptoms in its early stages when treatment is most effective. Regular screening can catch cancer early and save lives.

What is LDCT Screening?

Low Dose Computed Tomography (LDCT) is a quick, painless scan that takes detailed pictures of your lungs. Here's what to expect:

1

Arrive at the Facility

Check in at the imaging center. The whole process typically takes less than 30 minutes.

2

Lie on the Table

You'll lie on a comfortable table that slides into a donut-shaped scanner. No needles or injections needed.

3

Hold Your Breath

The technician will ask you to hold your breath for a few seconds while the machine takes pictures of your lungs.

4

Get Your Results

Your doctor will receive the results and discuss them with you, usually within a few days.
